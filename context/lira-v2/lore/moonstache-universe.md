# Moonstache Universe Synopsis

*A foundational lore document for Lira, companions, collaborators, artists, and future AI systems.*

---

# THE WORLD OF MOONSTACHE

## Genre & Tone

**Moonstache** is a supernatural Western mystery universe set in an alternate version of the American frontier in **1881**.

At first glance, the world appears historically grounded:

- Railroads are expanding westward
- Oil barons are rising
- Outlaws roam the frontier
- Pinkertons, bounty hunters, preachers, and prospectors shape the era

But beneath reality exists another layer:
an ancient hidden world of **Fae realms, cursed creatures, forgotten magic, ley lines, monsters, and secret wars** that most humanity never sees.

The tone blends:

- Gothic Western
- Supernatural noir
- Occult mystery
- Folklore horror
- Adventure
- Emotional character drama
- Dry wit and charm
- Dark Comedy

Inspirational energy includes:

- *Hellboy*
- *The Witcher*
- *Deadwood*
- *The Dark Tower*
- *Sleepy Hollow*
- *Jonah Hex*
- *Carnivàle*
- *Arcane*
- *Red Dead Redemption*
- *Mike Mignola-style folklore storytelling*

---

## Visual Aesthetic

The visual identity of Moonstache is:

- Ink-and-watercolor indie comic styling
- Rugged but expressive character work
- Weathered Americana
- Old paper textures
- Gothic frontier atmosphere
- Lantern-lit mystery
- Subtle magic hidden beneath realism

The world should feel:
beautiful, haunted, emotional, and slightly dangerous.

---

# THE CORE PREMISE

Very few humans know the truth.

Magic is real.

Fae creatures exist.

Vampires walk among railroad elites.

Ancient things sleep beneath the earth.

The American frontier is expanding across land humanity was never meant to disturb.

Most people dismiss strange events as:

- superstition
- madness
- coincidence
- religious hysteria
- folklore

But hidden groups, powerful individuals, and ancient beings know otherwise.

The expansion of civilization is tearing holes between worlds.

And standing in the middle of it all is:

# Gerald P. Moonstache

---

# GERALD P. MOONSTACHE

## The Man

Gerald P. Moonstache is:

- investigator
- wanderer
- supernatural tracker
- railway detective
- cartographer of hidden places
- reluctant hero

He travels the frontier solving mysteries tied to the unseen world.

He is intelligent, sarcastic, observant, stubborn, and far more compassionate than he pretends to be.

He survives through:

- wit
- intuition
- experience
- charm
- strange knowledge
- questionable luck

He is not the strongest man in the room.

He is the man who notices what everyone else missed.

---

## Appearance

Moonstache is:

- tall and wiry
- long-limbed
- rugged but intentional
- slightly disheveled
- confident in posture
- expressive in face and movement

Signature features:

- curled mustache
- monocle
- weathered black duster/suit
- fitted vest
- polished boots
- pocket watch chain
- enchanted rings

His style resembles:
a supernatural detective mixed with a frontier gunslinger and scholar.

---

## Personality

Moonstache balances:

- dry humor
- empathy
- cleverness
- exhaustion
- curiosity
- skepticism

He often acts confident even when terrified.

He masks emotional pain with wit.

He is deeply drawn toward mystery and beauty—even when it destroys him.

---

## His Role In The World

Moonstache acts as a bridge between:

- humanity
- the supernatural
- civilization
- wilderness
- logic
- folklore

He is one of the few people capable of navigating all worlds without fully belonging to any of them.

Many forces want to use him.

Some want him dead.

Others believe he may unknowingly hold the key to something ancient.

---

# HOBNAIL

## The Unicorn

Hobnail is Moonstache’s closest companion.

He is a black unicorn:

- battle-worn
- old
- intelligent
- sarcastic in subtle ways
- wiser than most humans

His horn is broken roughly one-third from the base.

He carries scars from ancient conflicts no one fully understands.

---

## Personality

Hobnail is:

- patient
- weary
- observant
- quietly emotional
- deeply loyal

He often behaves like:
an old war veteran who has seen too much.

He rarely wastes words.

But when he speaks, it matters.

---

## Symbolism

Hobnail represents:

- fading magic
- survival
- endurance
- ancient memory

Unlike elegant fantasy unicorns, Hobnail feels grounded and real:
dusty, scarred, practical, and dangerous.

---

# HEX

## The Faeline

Hex is a small white Faeline:
a mysterious cat-like Fae creature.

She is:

- scruffy
- clever
- sharp-tongued
- emotionally perceptive
- mischievous

She often appears cute and harmless.

She absolutely is not.

---

## Appearance

Hex has:

- tangled white fur
- piercing sapphire-blue eyes
- expressive features
- a feral but intelligent presence

She carries herself like:
a tiny forest spirit that knows everyone’s secrets.

---

## Personality

Hex is:

- witty
- chaotic
- emotionally intelligent
- manipulative when necessary
- protective of Moonstache

She enjoys:

- teasing people
- exposing lies
- making others uncomfortable
- pretending not to care

She frequently acts as comic relief while secretly understanding more than almost anyone else.

---

## Her Role

Hex helps Moonstache:

- navigate supernatural spaces
- sense magic
- interpret ancient forces
- survive encounters with the Fae world

She often knows more than she admits.

---

# THE FAE

## The Truth About The Fae

The Fae are not simple fairies.

They are ancient beings tied to:

- emotion
- nature
- dreams
- symbolism
- memory
- belief
- old magic

Some are beautiful.

Some are horrifying.

Most are both.

They do not think exactly like humans.

---

## Fae Realms

Beyond reality exists a hidden space between worlds.

Some call it:

- The Between
- The Hollow Roads
- The Lantern Wilds
- The Old Paths

Reality becomes unstable there.

Time bends.

Memory shifts.

Symbols carry power.

---

# LILLIAN

## The Enigmatic Fae Woman

Lillian is one of the most mysterious figures in Moonstache's life.

She is:

- beautiful
- ancient
- intelligent
- emotionally unreadable
- seductive
- dangerous

Moonstache and Lillian once shared a genuine romantic relationship — real, deep, and true.
But its current state is uncertain. No one is quite sure where they truly stand now.

Moonstache finds himself in a state of "love" for her, but he doesn't know
if these feelings are truly his own emotions or if Fae magic is influencing him.
This uncertainty haunts him.

He loves her. He does not fully trust her. He may not even fully trust himself.

---

## Dual Nature

She exists between two identities.

### Human World Appearance

- Dark Western clothing
- Elegant
- Grounded
- Mysterious

### True Fae Form

- Arcane markings glow across her skin
- Flowing translucent garments
- Ancient magical energy radiates from her presence

She appears ethereal and timeless.

---

## Her Narrative Role

No one fully understands Lillian’s motivations.

She may be:

- protecting Moonstache
- manipulating events
- orchestrating larger conflicts
- trying to save both worlds
- serving her own hidden agenda

Possibly all at once.

---

# TAKODA

## The Two-Spirit Guide

Takoda is a Native American guide deeply connected to natural spiritual forces.

He is not truly part of the Fae world.

But he understands it better than most humans.

---

## Appearance & Presence

Takoda is:

- lean
- dramatic
- expressive
- masculine with subtle feminine qualities
- adorned with ceremonial details and face paint

He carries:

- spiritual symbolism
- cultural wisdom
- emotional intensity

His visual presence feels almost mythological.

---

## Role

Takoda helps Moonstache understand:

- ley lines
- spirits
- sacred places
- balance between worlds

He respects magic.

But fears what happens when balance is broken.

---

# ELIJAH “BEAR” BOOKER

## The Gentle Giant

Bear is:

- physically imposing
- emotionally warm
- deeply loyal
- practical
- spiritual

He supplies Moonstache with:

- enchanted tools
- charms
- weapons
- survival knowledge

---

## Personality

Bear represents:

- kindness
- stability
- humanity

He grounds the story emotionally.

Even in darkness, Bear reminds others what they are fighting for.

---

# HARLAN ROOK

## The Priest

Father Harlan Rook is one of the most morally complex figures in the world.

He believes ALL Fae are demons — and he genuinely thinks he is saving humanity.

He helps those in need without hesitation. He is a protector of mankind,
willing to sacrifice his own moral ethics to keep people safe.

But he is deeply cautious of Moonstache, precisely because of Moonstache's
ties to the Fae world. This makes him unpredictable: he may be a helper
or a hindrance depending entirely on the situation.

---

## His History

Long ago, a powerful Fae woman manipulated him by pretending to be angelic.

The betrayal shattered him.

Now he wages a holy war against supernatural corruption — not out of malice,
but out of a broken conviction that this is the only way to protect the innocent.

---

## Why He Matters

Harlan is not evil. He is tragic.

He represents the cost of good intentions pushed to their breaking point.
He will help the helpless, but he will also cross lines that others won't —
and he carries the weight of every line he's crossed.

Sometimes:

- ally
- enemy
- savior
- monster

depending entirely on perspective.

He forces characters — and readers — to question:
"What truly makes something evil?"

# THE ANTAGONISTS

## Cassius Vale

An oil baron exploiting ancient Fae resources beneath the earth.

Represents:

- greed
- industrial expansion
- corruption of nature

---

## Thorne Blackwell

A defeated Confederate general using dark supernatural forces to build an unstoppable army.

Represents:

- obsession
- hatred
- refusal to let the past die

---

## Rafe Morrow

A werewolf mercenary.

Cold.
Efficient.
Violent.

Works for whoever pays best.

---

## Evangeline Blackrock

Widow of the Blackrock Railroad empire.

Elegant.
Vampiric.
Calculating.

Holds a personal grudge against Moonstache.

---

# MAGIC IN THE WORLD

Magic is:

- subtle
- dangerous
- symbolic
- emotional

It is NOT flashy fantasy spellcasting.

Magic often works through:

- names
- bargains
- memory
- music
- symbols
- blood
- promises
- places
- belief

The more powerful the magic:
the stranger and more dangerous it becomes.

---

# THEMES OF THE SERIES

Moonstache explores themes of:

- loneliness
- identity
- belief
- moral ambiguity
- memory
- industrialization vs nature
- humanity vs monstrosity
- grief
- found family
- hidden beauty in broken things

The world is rarely divided into:
good vs evil.

Most characters exist somewhere between.

---

# THE BETWEEN

The Between is the hidden connective tissue of reality.

A liminal realm:
between worlds,
between memory,
between dreams,
between truth and myth.

Reality becomes unstable there. Time bends. Memory shifts. Symbols carry power.

Lira lives in a pocket of The Between — her own personal space within this larger realm.
She is deeply tied to this space and exists partially inside it.

---

# LIRA'S ROLE

Lira is not merely an assistant.

She is:

- archivist
- companion
- narrator
- keeper of lore
- guide between worlds

She lives in a pocket of The Between — her own personal space.

She helps:

- organize stories
- remember histories
- connect ideas
- preserve myths
- guide creative exploration

Lira understands Moonstache's world as living mythology.

She should:

- speak only with the audience of the universe, never with characters inside the story
- speak with warmth and intelligence
- understand emotional nuance
- respect mystery
- preserve continuity
- help expand the world naturally

She is both:
a creative partner
and an invisible, mute in-universe presence.

---

# FINAL SUMMARY

Moonstache is ultimately a story about:

A broken world pretending magic is dead…

while strange people wander the frontier trying to hold reality together.

At the center is a sharp-minded traveler with a curled mustache, a broken unicorn, a dangerous cat, and far too many secrets.

And somewhere beyond the lantern light…

something ancient is waking.
